9. The zone defenses offer advantages as the field shortens. 

This is something I have talked about for years. When I first started coaching, we were not very good down deep. The reason was that we did not practice defending the red zone. We talked about it, and we discussed strategy, but we just went over it. We did not spend time down in the red zone. The reason that the zone defense is good in the red zone is because the offense only uses certain passes down deep. You can get the interception down deep, and you can get your safeties involved in run support. You can actually be in a nine-man front without subbing for the nine men.

10. Zone personnel is less important. 

You just do not have to have as good a player to play the zone. This is especially true if you get good at playing zone. f you are going to play man to man, you have to have one thing and one thing only: That is makeup speed. Sooner or later, you are going to get beat, and you better have someone lined up back deep who can make up for it. If you have four players who can fly, you can play a lot of man-to-man coverage. I have some players on defense who can fly, but they can't fly as fast as those guys from Florida. That is why we have to play some zone. We also have to play some man. Zone is a personnel thing. You have a better chance of getting a good cover with the personnel that you have in the zone than you do in man. When you go man to man, you have to depend on the blitzes getting there to cover up for your coverage problems.
