Here are our basic drills that we use to teach our techniques. We really stress these drills during the preseason. We do not run them as much in the regular season, but we do use them in warm-up. First is the shuffle drill. We go through the bags.

SHUFFLE

Next, we step over the bags. We do not cross over; we keep the shoulders square, the head up, and the tail down.

STEP OVER

The third drill is what we call fill and search. We shuffle and then step up and over, looking for the hole between the bags.

Next is the mirror or follow-the-man drill. One man tries to mirror the other as they go over the bags. We are working on footwork and agility on this drill.

Next we do a front-to-front or back-to-back find-the-man and form-tackle drill. We do not want anyone to get hurt in the drill. We have both men lie down on their backs and then have them get up on a signal. They get up and move down the line of bags.

Next is our work-up-the-field drill. We do not want them to cross over with the feet. We work upfield.

We do a lot of rope jumping during the off-season. This also helps us with our agility.
