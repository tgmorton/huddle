We used to get ready for a football season. Around the 4th of July we started getting ready. That is what we used to do. Now, in college, you go to year-round school. In June, you are in summer school. Why? You must have a good graduation rate. You better have players in condition. You better not get players hurt because you have only 85 scholarships. How do you do this? You put them in summer school in June and July. You have eight or nine weeks of lifting and running. You can get them in good shape. Say you go to a camp. Now we will get into some practical things. Let's review the work for the week. On Monday we do footwork, we do core work, and we do what we call pre-hab, which we get from the trainers and the physical therapist. Now, if we can do certain exercises before they get hurt, perhaps they will not get hurt, or if they do get hurt, the injury will not be as bad and they will recover from the injury more quickly. We finish our warm-up with loosening. I will cover all of this really quickly. Everyone knows what the speed-agility ladder is. We do that at least once a week. We do all kinds of drills. The players even make up drills. We make it fun for them, but we make it fast. We finish with that type of activity. Then they are jazzed up and ready to go.

Another thing we do is called line hops. We take tape and put it on the floor, or we will take a stripe on the floor or on the field, and we do little hops. It is basic plyometrics, but it is also knee and ankle rehab. We take a spot, and we hop to it. We hop forward, and we hop backward. We hop staying on the stripe. We go zigzag forward and then backward, staying off the stripe. We go laterally. We just keep going back and forth. We do hammer: "Don't touch the stripe moving forward." When they get good, we have them move the hands inside,just as a sprinter does. We are always coaching sprint technique.

When they get bored with that, we go a third of the way down the stripe and put a dot, line, or mark. We go another third of the stripe and put a dot, line, or mark. It is the same drills, but now we have them pause at the dots and stick it or touch it. Why is that important? If I am not a knee bender, I cannot play football. If I am not a knee bender, I can't stop. If I am not a knee bender, that is how I blow out my anterior cruciate ligament. We use this especially with our big guys who have big quads.
