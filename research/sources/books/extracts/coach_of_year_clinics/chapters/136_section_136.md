Next, I want to talk about our offensive philosophy. We came up with this in 1986, when I was the head coach at Copperas Cove High School in Texas. We had 1500 students in our school. They had won 14 games in 10 years. The next smallest school that we had on our schedule had over 2000 students. We were outmanned. On top of that, all of our kids lived around Ft. Hood. At that time it was the largest military base in the free world. We would get the kids in the ninth grade on up. We came up with this philosophy. We had seen Brigham Young University use this system, and it worked. We felt if we could teach this to high school students, we could teach it to anyone. We did it as an experiment, and it worked out. We have been doing it ever since. Our one basic statement about our philosophy would be this: Throw the short pass as many times as possible to people who score.

In the three years at Copperas Cove, we won 14 games. We beat four schools that the school had never beaten. We made the kids and the community proud of the program. We did not win the championship, basically because we were outmanned. In three years we had one Division I football player to sign a scholarship. By comparison, most of the schools we played against signed four or five Division I players per year.

We told our players that we could not do a lot of things well. We were not very big, and we did not have a lot of numbers. We convinced our kids that we could do this: We could throw the ball better than anyone in the state of Texas. The kids got excited about it. We did everything we could to promote that concept. We did it over and over again in terms of promotion and talking about it. We tried to pick out of our younger grades the skilled people. You can find them if you will look.

When we were at Copperas Cove, we had to coach both sides of the ball. Mike Majors, who is our defensive coordinator, coached our offensive line at Copperas Cove. He helped develop a lot of this offense. We just talked about these skilled people all of the time. We wanted to know who the players were who came along who could score. Who were the guys who were coming along who had a knack for scoring?

We identified the players who we felt could score. Then we put them in a position to score.
