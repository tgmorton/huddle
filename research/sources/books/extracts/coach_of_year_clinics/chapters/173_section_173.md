We are scooping really hard on the back side.

48 POWER SWEEP VS. 50 DEFENSE

As I said, we do throw the football. We run our combo pass protection for our one-step and three-step passing games. We do not release our running backs on this play. We pack it inside. We want to protect the quarterback. Teams are using the blitz more today. We do lock on man, or big on big. The backs block the first man to show outside of our tackle's block. If we get against a team that runs a slant-and-loop tackle, then we will have to keep our tight end in and let him block the second man outside. Our running back can't block both of them. That is our quick-step passing game.

COMBO PROTECTION

We were a sprint-out team this year because we had an outstanding quarterback who could run, and he could throw on the run. We changed our sprint protection this year. We decided that we would have our linemen take a short step inside and block the area. We used to turn inside, but we were getting too much penetration. We decided to step inside and keep our shoulders square.

We never take a man. We can't take care of the blitzes if we take a man. The backs take the first and second men outside of the play-side tackle.
