## The Route Tree (0-9)

The standard NFL route tree assigns numbers to routes based on depth and direction:

| # | Route | Depth | Direction | Description |
|---|-------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 0 | Hitch | 5 yds | Stop | Run 5, stop and turn back |
| 1 | Flat | 2 yds | Outside | Release to flat area |
| 2 | Slant | 5 yds | Inside | 3-step, break inside at 45째 |
| 3 | Comeback | 15 yds | Outside | Push vertical, come back to sideline |
| 4 | Curl | 12 yds | Inside | Push vertical, curl back toward QB |
| 5 | Out | 12 yds | Outside | Push vertical, break hard to sideline |
| 6 | Dig/In | 12 yds | Inside | Push vertical, break inside across field |
| 7 | Corner | 15 yds | Outside | Push vertical, break to pylon at 45째 |
| 8 | Post | 15 yds | Inside | Push vertical, break to goalpost at 45째 |
| 9 | Go/Fade | 30 yds | Vertical | Straight vertical, beat defender deep |

**Pattern:** Even numbers break inside, odd numbers break outside.

## Additional Routes (21)

Beyond the route tree, these specialized routes expand the playbook:

### Quick Routes
- **Bubble** - Immediate behind LOS toward sideline
- **Quick Out** - 3-step out, immediate sideline break
- **Speed Out** - Fast 5-yard out
- **Arrow** - RB 45째 release to flat
- **Swing** - RB swing to flat, width before depth
- **Drag** - Shallow cross at 3-5 yards

### Intermediate Routes
- **Stick** - 6-yard hitch settling in zone hole
- **Angle** - Release outside, break back inside
- **Whip** - Release inside, whip back outside
- **Pivot** - Short out, pivot back inside
- **Under** - Medium cross at 8 yards
- **Over** - Deep cross at 15 yards
- **Option** - Read coverage, break based on defender
- **Texas** - RB angle route, inside then flat

### Deep Routes
- **Seam** - Vertical up the hash marks
- **Wheel** - Release to flat, turn upfield along sideline
- **Sail** - Deep out at 18 yards

### Double Moves
- **Sluggo** - Fake slant, go vertical (slant-and-go)
- **Post-Corner** - Fake post, break to corner
- **Out-and-Up** - Fake out, go vertical

### Special
- **Screen** - Catch behind LOS with blockers

## Coverage Beaters Quick Reference

### vs Cover 0/1 (Man)
- Mesh, Double Slants, Y-Cross, PA Deep Cross
- Screens (beat blitz)
- China (mismatch creation)

### vs Cover 2
- Smash, Four Verts, Hitch-Seam, Post-Corner
- Seam routes split safeties
- Corner routes attack the hole

### vs Cover 3
- Slant-Flat, Stick, Flood, Dagger, Mills
- PA Boot, PA Post
- RPO Bubble (if box loaded)

### vs Cover 4 (Quarters)
- Smash, Four Verts, Scissors
- Intermediate routes (zones soft underneath)

### vs Zone (General)
- Spacing, Levels, Curl-Flat
- Find the void, work windows

## Implementation Notes

### For Simulation
Each concept should include:
1. **Route waypoints** for all receivers
2. **Read progression** (which receiver to look at first)
3. **Coverage key** (what defender determines the read)
4. **Timing** (when routes break, when ball should come out)

### For Play Calling AI
1. **Situation awareness** - Down, distance, score
2. **Coverage recognition** - Pre-snap read, coverage beater
3. **Tendencies** - Don't be predictable
4. **Personnel matchups** - Exploit advantages

### Missing from Current Implementation
- Double moves (sluggo, post-corner, out-and-up)
- Option routes (adjust based on coverage)
- Screen blocking assignments
- RPO mesh points and read keys
- Play action fake timing
