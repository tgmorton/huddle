## Context

I've been reviewing the existing `docs/UI_UX_DESIGN.md` and the current frontend implementation. The **conceptual foundation is excellent**:

- "Coach's desk" metaphor
- Split focus model (can't see everything at once)
- Event-driven gameplay
- "Weight over speed" philosophy
- "Stories over stats"
- Phase-aware context

But the **visual execution** is fairly standard dark-theme UI with generic choices (Inter font, typical color palette, conventional layouts). Given how distinctive the game design is, the visuals should match that ambition.

---

## The Opportunity

NFL Head Coach 09 had a memorable visual identity - the war room, the playbook aesthetics, the broadcast-style presentations. Our game's UI could be equally distinctive.

**Current visual identity:** Generic dark dashboard
**Potential visual identity:** _Something that feels like stepping into an NFL facility_

---

## Bold Directions to Consider

### Option A: War Room / Film Room Aesthetic

Think: Dim lighting, multiple screens, hand-drawn plays, preparing for battle.

**Visual Elements:**
- Chalkboard/whiteboard textures for play diagrams
- Dim ambient lighting with focused spotlights on content
- Multiple "monitor" frames showing different information
- Hand-drawn X's and O's as decorative elements
- Film grain and scan lines for atmosphere
- Typography: Bold condensed sans-serif (like actual NFL playbooks)

**Tone:** Intense, focused, professional. You're in the war room at 2 AM.

---

### Option B: Broadcast Graphics Aesthetic

Think: ESPN/NBC Sunday Night Football graphics package.

**Visual Elements:**
- Bold geometric shapes and angular containers
- Dynamic lower-thirds for player info
- Scoreboard-style stat presentations
- Team color gradients as accent elements
- Motion graphics language (staggered reveals, slides)
- Typography: Bold display fonts, stat numbers that pop

**Tone:** Polished, exciting, big-time. You're running a primetime operation.

---

### Option C: Editorial / Sports Illustrated Aesthetic

Think: Long-form sports journalism, beautiful photography, storytelling.

**Visual Elements:**
- Dramatic photography (player portraits with depth)
- Elegant serif typography for narratives
- Magazine-style layouts with pull quotes
- White space as a design element
- Story-first information hierarchy
- Typography: Mix of refined serif (stories) and bold sans (data)

**Tone:** Thoughtful, narrative, prestige. Players are people with stories.

---

### Option D: Utilitarian Playbook Aesthetic

Think: Actual coach's clipboard, handwritten notes, physical artifacts.

**Visual Elements:**
- Paper textures, graph paper backgrounds
- Handwriting fonts for notes and annotations
- Clipboard frames, binder ring holes, paper edges
- Rubber stamp effects for approvals/decisions
- Coffee stain rings (subtle texture)
- Typography: Typewriter, hand-lettered, stencil

**Tone:** Tactile, authentic, personal. This is YOUR playbook.

## What I Need From You

1. **Your gut reaction** - Which direction resonates with the game's soul?

## My Initial Instinct

I'm leaning toward **War Room + Broadcast hybrid**:

- **Management shell**: War room aesthetic (the planning/decision space)
- **Game day**: Broadcast aesthetic (the performance/spectacle)
- **Player cards**: Editorial touches (the human stories)

This creates visual rhythm - your weekly life is in the war room, but game day transforms into something more electric.
